How is this calculated?

Estimate = Assessed Value × 0.427 ÷ 100

$500,000 × 0.427 ÷ 100 = $2,135.00

0.427 is the most recently published municipal (local-purpose) tax rate per $100 of assessed value, used in the township's 2026 levy-cap calculation (Sheet 3-Levy CAP). The final 2026 rate depends on the certified tax levy and total assessed valuation, so treat this as an estimate, not a bill.

Where the 2026 budget goes

Staying within state limits

Under both New Jersey spending caps

New Jersey law limits how much most municipal spending (the appropriation “CAP”) and the property-tax levy (the levy cap) can grow each year. The adopted 2026 budget comes in under both.

Methodology

About this data

Every figure on this page is transcribed directly from the Township of West Windsor's adopted 2026 municipal budget (Mercer County, NJ), as filed with the New Jersey Division of Local Government Services.

  • Values are copied from the source workbook without recalculation.
  • Blank fields in the original budget are left blank here too — never replaced with zero.
  • Original department labels, FCOA codes, and source-sheet references are preserved for every row.
  • Spending totals shown in charts exclude the form's internal subtotal/total rows to avoid double-counting; the full Explorer table includes them, clearly labeled.
What about school taxes? This page covers only the municipal budget — the township government's own operations (police, roads, parks, etc.). School funding is a separate budget set by the West Windsor-Plainsboro Regional School District, and it's the largest part of most residents' property tax bills: school taxes for 2025 totaled $111,688,841, roughly twice the size of this entire municipal budget.

This tool is built and maintained by West Windsor Forward, a nonpartisan civic organization. It is not an official Township of West Windsor publication and does not replace the adopted budget document.
